It began, as the finest things do, with a refusal. A refusal to add anything. No glaze, no engraving, no gold leaf to apologize for what it is.

Every Imperial Brick is drawn from a single clay deposit, fired at exactly 1,000°C in a kiln used for nothing else, and left to cool for nine days under conditions our master has declined to disclose. The result is a red so dense it seems to hold its own shadow.

We do not sell bricks. We release them — slowly, rarely, and only to people who understand that the rarest luxury left in the world is something that refuses to be more than what it is.

There were never more than twelve. Five have already found permanent homes — one in a private vault in Geneva, one on a collector's mantel in Kyoto. Seven remain. There will not be an eighth.
